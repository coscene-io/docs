1. Product Overview

1.1 Product Overview

The Moosu robot positioning and navigation system is a positioning and navigation system that integrates laser radar, vision (binocular camera, depth camera), ultrasonic, inertial measurement unit (IMU) and other sensors. It integrates sensor technology, data processing, visual processing, automation control and other technologies, It solves the problems of robot perception of the environment, positioning, navigation, and obstacle avoidance. Machine vision based on deep learning can enable robots to detect and track pedestrians and moving targets. Combined with traditional image recognition, it can also help robots accurately and efficiently identify the status of field equipment and meter readings in real time, which is beneficial to security/logistics/power inspection robots. Complete tasks efficiently and accurately. In conjunction with the inspection robot solution, Mooshi Technology provides a management and monitoring platform as well as PC clients and Android clients, which have been developed specifically for gas, electricity, oil fields, chemical and other industries. The visual processing, positioning and navigation system of the eyesight robot has been adapted to various types of wheeled and tracked robot chassis, EAI, KOBUKI and other robot cars, as well as various types of radars such as Shenzhen Leishen, Silan RPLIDAR, German SICK, American Velodyne and so on. Equipped, it can be easily used in logistics/security/ In the electric power/gas/oil field/chemical industry, related products have been commercially deployed in oil fields, gas, electric power, chemical industry, etc. The eyesight robot has the following characteristics: vision processing, positioning and navigation system:

  1. Good modularity and easy to integrate with existing mobile robot systems;

  2. Low cost and high accuracy (relative error 0.3%, absolute error plus or minus 1 cm);

  3. Supports dynamic environments and can intelligently avoid obstacles and pedestrians;

  4. Precise remote control, supporting mobile client and Web client;

  5. A complete management and monitoring platform to support customers’ cloud platform management;

  6. Support various mobile robot chassis;

  7. Supports various sensors and the fusion of multiple sensors (IMU+vision, IMU+lidar, lidar+vision, etc.);

  8. Supports commercial embedded hardware and has good compatibility;

  9. Provide overall solutions including chassis, embedded hardware, SLAM software, machine vision software, management and monitoring platform, Web client, and Android client.

1.2 Crawler robot

  1. The support area is large, the ratio of the 150mm wide crawler track to the ground length and the length of the entire vehicle reaches 75%, and the grounding specific pressure is 12KPa (full load). It is suitable for working in complex environments such as soft and muddy environments. It has small sag, small rolling resistance and good off-road maneuverability.

  2. The turning radius is extremely small and can be turned in place.

  3. There are crawler teeth on the support surface of the crawler track, which are not easy to slip and have good traction adhesion performance, which is conducive to exerting greater traction force.

  4. The chassis height exceeds 130mm and has super strong climbing and obstacle climbing capabilities.

  5. The car body shell is made of 304 stainless steel, which is strong and durable and has good overall protective performance.

1.3 Wheeled robot

  1. Using brushless DC motor + reducer + solid rubber wheel, it has strong power and good passability.

  2. Four-wheel differential steering, capable of turning 360 degrees on the spot, with flexible maneuverability and small turning space.

  3. Same-side co-drive, when one wheel on one side is suspended in the air, all power is output to the other wheel on the same side to avoid insufficient power in the suspension.

  4. Optional large-capacity lithium battery has a maximum battery life of more than 4 hours and a maximum charging time of 2 hours (fully charged).

  5. Travel speed 0-4.5Km/h adjustable, precise control.

2. Start the robot

2.1 Start the robot

Press the button in the middle of the picture control panel. After powering on, the button indicator lights up and the power display panel displays the current power level. Before starting up After booting

2.2 Remote control use

After the robot is powered on, push up the switch in the middle of the remote control, turn on the remote control, and automatically connect to the robot. Push the right joystick up and down to control forward and backward movement, and push left and right to control left and right movement. See the figure below for details. Remote control operation button instructions Note: When starting to control the car, use less force on the remote control to prevent the robot from moving too fast.

2.3 Advanced use of remote control

For advanced users, other buttons on the remote control are described as follows:

  • Green on the left: Throttle control, the default is down and low

  • Green on the right: Direction control, which controls the forward, backward, left and right movements of the robot respectively.

  • Red in the middle: power switch, push it up to power on;

  • Blue on both sides of the middle: respectively throttle and direction control trim switches, used to control signal balance adjustment

  • Yellow on both sides of the middle: respectively the throttle and directional motion compensation switches, used to control signal balance adjustment

  • Blue below: Controller and robot connection status display

2.4 Notes

  1. If the car is non-waterproof and dustproof, it is prohibited to use it in harsh environments with moisture, rain, and lots of dust. The working environment temperature should be within the range of -20℃ ~ 55℃.

  2. The robot should avoid contact with flammable, explosive, corrosive liquids and gases.

  3. It is necessary to understand the vehicle body’s load-carrying, hill-climbing, obstacle-crossing and other performance, and use it within the design parameters. Otherwise, the service life of the machine will be reduced and there will be a risk of overturning.

  4. Before starting the car, you should conduct a test run to check the battery power, whether the emergency stop switch, instruments, etc. can work normally, whether the car's steering is flexible, whether the steering handle is normal, and whether the brakes are normal.

  5. The starting speed should not be too fast. Make sure that there are no people or obstacles within the moving range of the car. Gradually adjust the speed from slow to fast to avoid injuries or losses caused by sudden changes in speed.

  6. When the car is climbing a slope or moving forward with a load, the speed should be reduced as much as possible to prevent the motor from overloading.

Preparation: Select the initial position of the drawing and paste the QR code (QR code with any ID other than 6/18) at the same height as the visible light camera.

4.1 Drawing

  1. After connecting the Android tablet, click "Remote Control Function" on the main menu interface. It is recommended that the linear speed is not greater than 0.3m/s and the angular speed is not greater than 0.3m/s. Not greater than 0.3rad/s. By going up, down, left and right, the remote control car goes directly in front of the QR code.

  2. For Android tablets, return to the main menu -> Cartography navigation function -> Cartography function -> Create a new map and enter remote control Graph mode interface.

  3. After waiting for the coordinates to be displayed, remote control the car. It is recommended that the linear speed is 0.3m/s and the angular speed is 0.3rad/s. Control the car first. Spin in place. Within the route that needs to be mapped, the car makes a round trip. After covering the map, the car is moved to the front of the QR code again at the end of the cartography, and the distance from the QR code is farther than when the cartography started.

  4. Click "Drawing" in the lower right corner of the "Remote Control Drawing Mode" interface to complete drawing. After the mapping is completed, enter the map editing interface, enter 0.5 for the trajectory expansion radius, and click "Execute trajectory editing"

4.4 Notes

4.4.1 Preparation before drawing

  1. Before mapping, you should determine whether the previous map is still needed and whether it needs to be backed up.

  2. Try to scan when there are no people in the area to be scanned, or when there are fewer people.

  3. Try to remove frequently moving objects in the area to be scanned.

  4. Paste the QR code in places where you pass it frequently.

  5. For reflective, light-transmitting, light-absorbing and other objects in the scene, try to place them on the robot when conditions permit. Attach other materials that do not affect the normal scanning of the radar to the radar scanning plane (within 4cm above and below the radar height). (Objects with low reflectivity, such as black light-absorbing walls, furniture, etc. Glass mirrors, stainless steel and other specular reflective objects)

4.4.2 Principles of mapping operation

Use rviz during the mapping process, roslaunch rcp_slam carto_rviz.launch, to view the mapping effect. During the mapping process, it is recommended to follow the following principles:

  1. When drawing, try to ask the robot to walk in the middle of the road. On straight roads, try to ask the robot to make fewer turns and walk less close to obstacles.

  2. When the robot turns or turns around a corner, it does not complete the turn at once. You can turn 30 degrees and walk a little forward, turn another 30 degrees and walk a little forward. (Note: Especially when the road roughness is large and the angular velocity is small)

  3. For linear maps, use a 360-degree laser and make one trip (if the mapping effect is not good, you will go back and forth during the drawing); use a non-360-degree laser to make the same journey.

  4. The operator stands directly behind the robot.

  5. When drawing starts, make one turn at the origin (one turn is recommended for both 360-degree and non-360-degree drawings).

  6. When passing the QR code for the first time, the robot will be closer to the QR code, and when it encounters the QR code for the second time, it will be farther away from the QR code. When the car passes the QR code, ask the robot to look at the QR code

  7. Use a lower speed, it is recommended to be less than 0.5m/s. The speed depends on the current platform performance.

  8. Take more loops that create closed loops.

  9. After the loop returns to the origin, keep the robot moving and take more overlapping paths. Do not stop moving immediately.

  10. After the loop returns to the origin, if the map is not closed (view RVIZ during the mapping process, as shown below), continue walking (using a smaller driving speed, such as 0.3m/s) until it is closed.

  11. For closed areas, avoid taking the same old path and reduce memory consumption.

4.4.3 Checking the mapping results

After the map construction is completed, it is necessary to judge whether the built map meets the requirements. Here's how to do it:

  1. Restart the machine;

  2. Use RVIZ, command: roslaunch rcp_slam nav_rviz.launch, to view the map;

  3. There are the following inspection principles:

    1. Are the edges of obstacles in the map clear?

    2. Are there areas in the map that are inconsistent with the actual environment (whether there are closed loops).

    3. Is there a gray area within the robot’s action area on the map?

    4. Whether there are obstacles in the map that will not exist during subsequent positioning (people walking when scanning the map, moving objects).

    5. Whether it can be ensured that the robot has a 360-degree field of view at any position within the activity area.

5. Navigation

In the main interface of the robot control terminal, go to Cartography Navigation Mode -> Navigation/Visual Tracking -> Navigation Function, and wait for the map to be loaded. After loading the map, the map is gray until the relocation is successful. Through the control function of this interface, you can control the robot to the initial drawing position, rotate the robot, and quickly reposition it. Success, after the relocation is successful, there will no longer be a gray layer on the map. You can see that the location on the map where the robot walked while drawing is white.

5.1 Navigate to a specified point

At any point on the road that the robot has walked (white area), long press and select "Start", and the robot can navigate to this point autonomously. If the robot does not move after clicking on the target location, it means that the location cannot be reached due to physical restrictions. The specific reasons are:

  1. The target space is too small, causing the robot to be unable to approach the location;

  2. The target point is very close to the obstacle;

  3. Impossible to walk due to roadblocks;

  4. Ultrasonic obstacle stop: Ultrasonic waves detect obstacles at close range;

  5. The robot is surrounded by obstacles;

5.2 Navigate to specified coordinates

On the main interface of the robot control terminal, go to Cartography Navigation Mode -> Navigation/Visual Tracking -> Navigation Function. After the relocation is successful, click "Set Target". After setting the coordinates of the target point, click "Confirm" and the robot will automatically navigate to Target.

5.3 Cancel navigation

During the navigation process, click "Cancel Navigation" on the map information interface, and the robot will stop moving immediately.

9. Precautions

Inspection robots often work in harsh environments, so regular inspection and maintenance of the robots can not only extend the service life of the robots, but also detect potential faults early, deal with them early, and reduce maintenance time and costs.

9.1 Routine inspection

  1. Before the robot starts working, you should check the battery power of the robot to ensure that the charging function is normal. Start working after the battery is full to avoid being unable to complete the entire inspection process due to low battery power.

  2. When maintenance of production equipment is required on site, stop the inspection robot from performing tasks and handle the steps of normal shutdown and startup:

    1. The robot returns to the charging station and is fully charged

    2. Terminate all inspection tasks on the monitoring and management platform

    3. Manually shut down the robot to perform on-site operations

    4. When inspection is needed, manually turn on the robot

    5. On the monitoring and management platform, open the live broadcast, and the remote control robot moves forward about 30~50cm, resets the periodic tasks, and performs inspection tasks.

  3. After the inspection robot is used in an environment contaminated with sand, gravel, oil or chemicals, it should promptly clean the wheels and check and clean up any stuck foreign objects to avoid damage to the tire life and chassis moving parts.

  4. During normal operation, the bolts and nuts of each part of the robot should be checked at least once a month. If there is any looseness, tighten it in time. The bolts of chassis moving parts should be inspected with emphasis. The movable hinge points, bearings and connections of the entire vehicle need to be lubricated in time and the working conditions must be checked. When you find paint peeling, bruises, etc., you should promptly Touch up the paint to prevent rust and affect the structural strength.

9.2 Maintenance

  1. The robot should be inspected every 100km it travels.

  2. When the operator is maintaining or inspecting the robot, other personnel are prohibited from operating the remote control. Otherwise, serious personal injury or equipment damage may occur.

  3. Before carrying out maintenance work, the commander of the operation should be determined, the operation process should be formulated, and the operation should be carried out in a planned and step-by-step manner.

  4. Please use correct and qualified maintenance tools. Do not use damaged or inferior tools to repair the robot.

  5. When disassembling the robot shell for maintenance, do not drop foreign objects inside the machine, otherwise it may cause machine damage, operating errors or other malfunctions.

  6. Maintenance of electrical systems should be performed by personnel with professional qualifications in low-voltage electrical .

  7. If you need to perform electric welding on the robot, you need to disconnect the external power supply and the battery, and have it performed by personnel with welding qualifications. Otherwise, the robot's electrical system may be damaged.

9.3 Long-term storage

  1. When the robot stops working for a long time, it should be stored in a dry, clean, and ventilated indoor environment to avoid being exposed to moisture, high heat, or exposure to the sun for a long time to avoid accelerated aging of the equipment.

  2. Before stopping work, the battery should be fully charged and then turned off and stored. The battery power should be checked at least once a month. If the power is less than 30%, it should be charged in time to avoid damage to the battery due to long-term over-discharge.

  3. When parking the robot, avoid parking on slopes or obstacles. The wheels on both sides should be stressed at the same time and land evenly to extend the service life of the tires.

Problem: The robot shows that the battery level is 0 and cannot be turned on Solution: When the robot is not used for a long time, it should be charged regularly to avoid over-discharging the battery and affecting its service life. If you encounter a situation where the battery is exhausted, please handle it as follows:

  1. Push the robot to the charging pile on a cart.

  2. Connect the charger to the power supply and manually insert it into the charging port of the robot (the robot remains powered off);

  3. Charge the robot for 3~4 hours, then press the power button to power on;

  4. After confirming that the battery power is within the normal range, insert the charging plug back into the charging pile. The remote-controlled robot returns to the charging pile and waits for the robot to perform its tasks normally. Problem: Bot is not online Solution: There may be the following situations:

  • Whether the cloud server has expired due to arrears, please renew in time.

  • Whether the robot's communication module 5G card is in arrears and is out of service, please renew it in time.

  • Check whether the LAN is working properly. Problem: The robot did not follow the scheduled inspection route, but finally arrived at the inspection point Solution: When the scheduled inspection route is blocked by obstacles, the robot will automatically plan a new route to bypass the obstacles. At this time, you should check whether there are any subtle obstacles on the scheduled inspection route, such as smooth and reflective water surfaces, oil stains, deep cracks and other obstacles that may interfere with the robot's progress.
